# UW Course Finder — Project Description

Planning your path to a University of Washington degree is harder than it should
be. The classes you need, the order you have to take them in, and the
prerequisites that block each one are scattered across catalogs, audits, and
dense department pages. Figuring out "what do I take, and when?" usually means
piecing it together by hand.

UW Course Finder makes that simple. Pick your major and the app instantly builds
a quarter-by-quarter plan that respects every prerequisite, so you always take
courses in a workable order. From there you can browse the full course catalog
and add, remove, or move courses between quarters — and your progress toward
graduation updates as you go.

A built-in prerequisite map shows how each class flows into the ones it unlocks,
so you can see the whole picture at a glance. Click any course for its credits,
prerequisites, and when it's offered.

Built on real UW course data for Computer Science, Informatics, Mathematics, and
Applied Math — and unlike a static DARS checklist or MyPlan's scheduler, it
sequences your whole path and maps it visually — turning a confusing process into
a clear, visual plan.
